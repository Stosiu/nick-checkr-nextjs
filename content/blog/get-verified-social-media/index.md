## What verification actually means

A verified badge tells other users that the platform has confirmed you are who you claim to be. That's it. It doesn't mean the platform endorses you, thinks you're important, or considers your content good.

In practice, verification does a few useful things: it prevents impersonation, it gives your profile more visibility in search results, and it signals to other users that your account is legitimate. On some platforms it also unlocks features like longer posts or priority support.

What it doesn't do: guarantee you followers, make your content go viral, or protect you from getting banned. The badge is a trust signal, not a status symbol, even though people treat it like one.

## Instagram and Meta Verified

[Instagram](/check/instagram) has two paths to verification in 2026.

### The free path (notable figures)

Instagram still offers free verification to public figures, celebrities, brands, and accounts with significant media coverage. You apply through the app: go to Settings, then Account, then Request Verification. You'll need to provide a government-issued photo ID and explain why your account qualifies as being in the public interest.

Meta reviews applications manually and rejects most of them. There's no follower threshold published, but in practice you need either substantial media coverage (articles from recognized publications mentioning you by name) or a demonstrable risk of impersonation. Being an influencer with 50,000 followers isn't enough by itself if nobody outside Instagram knows who you are.

If you're rejected, you can reapply after 30 days. Many accounts get rejected multiple times before being approved, or never get approved at all.

### Meta Verified ($14.99/month)

Launched in 2023, Meta Verified lets anyone buy a blue checkmark for $14.99/month on mobile or $11.99/month on the web. You still need to verify your identity with a government ID and a selfie video, so it's not entirely pay-to-play. You must be at least 18 and your account must match the name on your ID.

What you get beyond the badge: impersonation protection monitoring, access to live chat support with a real person, and exclusive stickers. Whether that's worth $180/year depends on your situation. If you're a small business owner who regularly deals with fake accounts using your name, the impersonation monitoring alone might justify it. If you just want the badge for credibility, it's a vanity expense.

One thing to keep in mind: your [username and display name](/blog/username-vs-display-name) both matter for verification. Meta Verified requires that your profile name matches your government ID. If you've been using a pseudonym or brand name as your display name, you'll need to change it.

## TikTok verification

[TikTok](/check/tiktok) verification is free, but you can't apply for it. The platform identifies accounts that it believes need verification and reaches out to them directly. There's no application form, no settings menu option, no secret link.

TikTok's criteria are vague by design. They look for accounts that are "notable" and at risk of impersonation. In practice, this means established creators with large followings, recognized brands, public figures, and accounts that are frequently impersonated. Having a million followers doesn't guarantee you'll get verified, and some accounts with under 100,000 followers have been verified because they're well-known outside TikTok.

What you can do to improve your chances:

- Build a presence outside TikTok (press coverage, other verified social accounts, a Wikipedia page)
- Keep your account active and consistently posting
- Make sure your profile is complete with a clear photo, bio, and link
- Don't change your username frequently, since it makes your account harder for TikTok to associate with your public identity
- If you need to change your handle, use a tool to [check if your new username is available](/check/tiktok) on TikTok and your other platforms before switching

There's no paid verification option on TikTok. Accounts that claim to sell TikTok verification are scams.

## Twitter/X verification

[Twitter](/check/twitter) scrapped its old verification system in 2023 and replaced it with X Premium (formerly Twitter Blue). The blue checkmark is now a subscription feature.

Pricing in 2026: $8/month on the web or $11/month on iOS/Android (Apple and Google take a cut). There's also a Premium+ tier at $16/month with additional features like no ads and the largest reply boost.

Unlike the old system, X Premium verification doesn't confirm you're a notable public figure. It confirms your phone number is verified, your account is older than 90 days, and you're paying. That's the bar. This means the blue checkmark on Twitter carries less weight than it used to.

What you get: the badge, ability to edit posts, longer posts (up to 25,000 characters on Premium+), reduced ads, and a boost in reply ranking so your replies appear higher in threads. The reply boost is probably the most practically useful feature if you're trying to grow an audience.

Organizations can get a gold checkmark through Verified Organizations, which starts at $200/month. This is separate from individual verification and includes the ability to affiliate employee accounts.

If you're [using the same username across platforms](/blog/same-username-everywhere), note that Twitter has a 15-character limit on usernames. That's shorter than most platforms and can force you into a different handle than you use elsewhere.

## YouTube verification

[YouTube](/check/youtube) gives verified badges to channels that meet two requirements: at least 100,000 subscribers and a complete channel (profile picture, banner, description, active content). There's no application. YouTube grants it automatically when you hit the threshold.

For channels representing a well-known artist, public figure, or company, YouTube may verify at lower subscriber counts. The main channel of a verified musician, for example, might get the badge well before 100,000 subscribers if they're established elsewhere.

YouTube's badge is purely a checkmark next to your channel name in search results and comments. It doesn't unlock additional features. The real milestone is the 100K subscriber Silver Play Button, which arrives as a physical plaque.

## Is paid verification worth it?

Depends on who you are.

If you're a business that regularly deals with impersonation or customer trust issues, Meta Verified's $15/month is reasonable. The support access alone can save you hours of frustration when something goes wrong with your account.

If you're an individual creator who just wants the badge to look credible, it's harder to justify. The checkmark used to mean "this person is notable enough that the platform verified them." Now it means "this person pays a subscription fee." Most people can tell the difference, and the prestige has diminished accordingly.

X Premium is a slightly better value proposition because of the functional benefits: reply boost, longer posts, reduced ads. You're paying for features, not just a badge. Whether those features matter depends on how you use the platform.

My honest take: spend the time and money on making your content better instead. A strong profile with consistent branding, a clean [username that matches across platforms](https://nickcheckr.com), and good content will do more for your credibility than a checkmark.

## Tips for getting verified (the free way)

For platforms where free verification still exists (Instagram's notable figure path, TikTok, YouTube):

- Get press coverage. Articles from recognized news outlets that mention you by name are the strongest signal. A Wikipedia page is even better.
- Be consistent. Use the same username and branding across platforms. When a platform's team looks you up, they should find a coherent identity, not a dozen mismatched accounts. If your [desired username is already taken](/blog/username-already-taken) on some platforms, that's worth resolving before you pursue verification.
- Complete your profile. Every field filled in, a clear profile photo that matches your other accounts, a bio that explains who you are.
- Don't buy followers. Platforms can detect artificial inflation and it will hurt your chances.
- Be patient. Verification isn't a growth hack. It's a consequence of building something worth verifying.
