## The short version

If you just need the steps: open Instagram, tap your profile picture, tap **Edit Profile**, tap the **Username** field, type your new username, and tap **Done** (or **Submit** on desktop). That's it.

But there are a few things worth knowing before you pull the trigger, especially if you use the same username across multiple platforms. Read on.

## How to change your Instagram username on mobile

This works the same on both iOS and Android.

1. Open the Instagram app
2. Tap your **profile picture** in the bottom-right corner
3. Tap **Edit profile**
4. Tap the **Username** field
5. Delete your current username and type the new one
6. Tap **Done** (iOS) or the **checkmark** (Android)

Instagram will tell you immediately if the name is taken. If it goes through, your new username is live right away. No confirmation email, no waiting period.

## How to change your Instagram username on desktop

1. Go to [instagram.com](https://instagram.com) and log in
2. Click your **profile picture** in the top-right, then click **Profile**
3. Click **Edit profile**
4. Find the **Username** field and change it
5. Click **Submit**

Same deal — instant feedback on availability, instant change if it works.

### Understand that your old username gets released immediately

This is the big one. The moment you change your Instagram username, your old one becomes available for anyone to register. There is no grace period. There is no reservation window. Someone — or some bot — can grab it within seconds.

If your old username has any value (it's short, it's a real word, it matches your brand), think carefully before giving it up.

### Update your links everywhere

Your old Instagram profile URL (`instagram.com/oldname`) will stop working after someone else claims that username. Even before that happens, it redirects to your new profile only temporarily. Update your:

- Website bio links and social icons
- Link-in-bio tools (Linktree, etc.)
- Business cards and printed materials
- Email signatures
- Other social media profiles that link to your Instagram

### Tell your audience

If you have any kind of following, give people a heads up before the switch. Post a story, put it in your bio for a few days, mention it in a post. People who search for your old handle won't find you otherwise.

### Think about SEO

If people Google your old username and find your Instagram, that stops working once you change. Google will eventually re-index your new profile, but it takes time. For most people this doesn't matter. If you're a business or public figure, factor it in.

## Instagram username rules

Before you get attached to a new name, here's what Instagram actually allows:

- **Length:** 1 to 30 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters (a-z), numbers (0-9), periods (.), and underscores (_)
- **Not allowed:** Spaces, hyphens, special characters, or any other symbols
- **Case-insensitive:** `CoolUser` and `cooluser` are the same username — you can't take a name that exists in a different case
- **Must be unique:** No two accounts can share a username

One quirk worth noting: you can change the capitalization of your own username without losing it. If you're `cooluser` and want to display as `CoolUser`, just change it — since the underlying name is the same, nobody else can snipe it during the switch.

## Your Threads username changes too

Since Meta linked Instagram and Threads accounts, your Threads username is always the same as your Instagram username. When you change one, the other changes automatically. There's no way around this. If you've built a separate audience on Threads, keep that in mind.

## Common problems (and fixes)

### "This username isn't available"

This could mean a few things:

- **Someone else has it.** The obvious one. You can check this by searching for the username on Instagram directly.
- **It's reserved.** Instagram reserves certain usernames, especially ones that match trademarks, celebrity names, or platform features. You won't be able to claim these even if no active account uses them.
- **It violates community guidelines.** Usernames that contain offensive terms or impersonate others get blocked automatically.

If the name you want is taken, we have a whole guide on [what to do when your username is taken](/blog/username-already-taken).

### "You can't change your username right now"

Instagram limits how often you can change your username. The exact limit isn't published, but if you've changed it more than twice in a short period, you might hit a temporary cooldown. Wait a few days and try again.

### The Username Shows as Available but Won't Save

This happens occasionally and is usually a caching issue on Instagram's side. A few things to try:

- Close and reopen the app
- Try on desktop instead of mobile (or vice versa)
- Wait an hour and try again
- Make sure your app is updated to the latest version

If it persists for more than a day, the username might actually be reserved or flagged in a way that isn't surfaced in the UI.

## Username vs. display name — they're different things

Your **username** (also called your handle) is the `@name` that appears in your profile URL and when people tag you. Your **display name** is the bold text at the top of your profile that can contain spaces, special characters, and even non-Latin scripts.

Changing your username is what this guide covers. Changing your display name is a separate field in the same Edit Profile screen, and it has much looser rules.
