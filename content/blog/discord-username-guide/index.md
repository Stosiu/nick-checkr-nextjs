## The old system is dead

Discord used to let multiple people share the same name. You'd be `Alex#4821` and someone else would be `Alex#0037`. The four-digit discriminator made each account unique, so the name itself didn't have to be.

That system is gone. In mid-2023, [Discord](/check/discord) rolled out a new username system that works like every other platform: one unique username per person, no discriminators. If you want `alex`, you need to be the one person on Discord who has it.

This was controversial when it was announced. Plenty of users liked the old system — it meant you could always be "Alex" without worrying about availability. But Discord's reasoning was straightforward: telling a friend to add you as `Alex#4821` was clunky. People forgot the numbers, mistyped them, confused zero with O. The new system gives you a single, clean handle that's easier to share.

## How the new usernames work

Your Discord username is now a unique, lowercase handle. It follows rules similar to what you'd find on [Twitter](/check/twitter) or [Reddit](/check/reddit), with a few Discord-specific quirks.

Here are the constraints:

- 2 to 32 characters long
- Lowercase letters (a-z), numbers (0-9), underscores (_), and periods (.) only
- No uppercase letters, no spaces, no special characters
- Cannot have two consecutive periods (..)
- Cannot start or end with a period
- Must be unique across all of Discord

If you're used to other platforms, the lowercase-only rule is the biggest adjustment. On Instagram you can display as `CoolUser` even though the underlying username is case-insensitive. Discord doesn't do that. Your username is stored and displayed in lowercase. Period. If you want mixed-case branding, that's what the display name is for.

## How to claim your username

If you haven't switched yet, Discord will prompt you when you open the app. You can also do it manually:

1. Open Discord (desktop or mobile)
2. Go to **User Settings** (the gear icon near your avatar)
3. Click on **My Account**
4. Under **Username**, click **Edit**
5. Enter your new username
6. Confirm with your password

Discord checks availability in real time. If the name is taken, you'll know immediately. Once you confirm, the change is instant and your old `Name#1234` identity is gone for good.

You get one free username change. After that, Discord limits how often you can change it — currently once every 30 days or so, though they haven't published an exact number.

## The three layers of Discord identity

Discord is unusual because it gives you three separate names, not two. Understanding the difference matters, and it trips up a lot of people. We covered this across platforms in our [username vs display name breakdown](/blog/username-vs-display-name), but here's the Discord-specific version.

Your username is the unique handle. It's what people search for when adding you as a friend, and it's the one identity that must be unique. Lowercase only, follows the rules above.

Your display name is a global name that appears in DMs, friend lists, and as a default in servers. It can be up to 32 characters, supports mixed case, spaces, special characters, and emoji. It doesn't need to be unique. This is where you put your brand name, real name, or whatever you want people to actually see.

Your server nickname is a per-server override. Admins can set it, or you can set your own (if the server allows it). It only applies in that specific server. You might be "Alex" in one server and "Raid Leader Alex" in another.

In practice, most people interact with your display name or server nickname. The username is mostly for friend requests, search, and account identity. But it's the one you can't change on a whim, so choose it carefully.

## What happened to old usernames

When Discord migrated accounts, they rolled it out in waves. Nitro subscribers and older accounts got first pick, which caused its own controversy. Here's what happened to the different pieces:

Your old display name (the part before the #) became your new display name. So if you were `CoolGamer#7291`, your display name defaulted to `CoolGamer`. You then had to pick a new unique username separately.

The discriminator (#7291) is just gone. It doesn't exist anywhere in the new system.

If you didn't claim a username during the rollout, Discord may have auto-assigned one based on your old name. Some people ended up with usernames like `coolgamer7291` — functional but not great. You can still change it.

## Tips for picking a Discord username

The good short usernames went fast. If you're still looking for the right one, a few things to keep in mind.

Check other platforms first. If you're going to be `coolname` on Discord, you probably want it on other platforms too. Run it through [NickCheckr](https://nickcheckr.com) to see where it's available before you lock it in on Discord. Our guide on [using the same username everywhere](/blog/same-username-everywhere) covers why this matters.

Keep it short. You have up to 32 characters, but shorter is better. People need to type it to add you as a friend. Something between 3 and 15 characters is the sweet spot.

Go easy on periods and underscores. `cool.name` is fine. `c.o.o.l.n.a.m.e` is not. Separators help readability but become noise when overused.

Don't rely on numbers as a crutch. Adding `123` or your birth year to the end of a taken name is technically a solution, but it looks like you settled. If the name you want is taken, try a genuinely different variation instead. We have a full guide on [what to do when your username is already taken](/blog/username-already-taken).

Think long-term. Discord limits username changes. Whatever you pick, you're likely living with it for a while. A gaming reference that's funny now might not age well. Your real name or a consistent brand handle tends to hold up better.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I use uppercase letters?** No. Discord usernames are strictly lowercase. Use your display name for mixed-case styling.

**Can I change my username back to my old one?** Only if nobody else has claimed it. Old names were released when the migration happened, and popular ones were taken quickly.

**Do my friends need to re-add me?** No. Your friend list, server memberships, DMs, and message history all stay intact. The username change is cosmetic from a connections standpoint.

**What if someone took my name?** You're out of luck unless they abandon it. Discord doesn't offer username disputes the way some platforms handle trademark claims. First come, first served.

**Is my username shown publicly?** It depends on context. In servers, people typically see your display name or server nickname. Your username is visible on your profile card and when someone searches for you.
