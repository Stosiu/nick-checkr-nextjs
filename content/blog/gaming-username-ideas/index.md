## How to actually pick one

A list of 250 names is useless if you can't narrow it down. Here's how to think about it.

Match the name to how you play. If you're a competitive grinder, a funny name might undercut you in tournament settings. If you mostly play casual with friends, an intimidating name is going to feel try-hard. Pick something that fits the version of you that shows up in game.

Say it out loud. You'll hear this name on Discord calls, in Twitch chat, maybe on a stream. "Vesper" rolls off the tongue. "DarkRuneKeeper" is a mouthful. This matters more than people think, especially if you ever plan to stream or join voice comms with strangers.

Check the length. Platform rules differ. Xbox gamertags max out at 12 characters. PSN allows 16. Steam is more lenient. If you want one name across everything, aim for 12 characters or fewer. Our breakdown of [username character limits](/blog/username-character-limits) covers the specifics for every major platform.

Avoid names that only work right now. Pop culture references date fast. A name that references a trending game or meme will feel stale in two years. Nobody wants to be stuck as "AmongUsImpostor" in 2027.

Claim it everywhere, not just on one platform. You might only play on Steam right now, but if you ever branch out to console or start streaming, you'll want that name waiting for you. The [same username everywhere](/blog/same-username-everywhere) approach saves future headaches. Register your chosen name on platforms you don't use yet. It takes two minutes and prevents someone else from squatting on it.
