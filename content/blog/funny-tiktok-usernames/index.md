## Before you commit to a name

A few things worth knowing before you change your TikTok username.

TikTok enforces a 30-day cooldown between username changes. If you pick something and hate it after a week, you're stuck for three more. Read the full [TikTok username rules](/blog/tiktok-username-rules) so you don't burn your one change on a typo.

Funny usernames are memorable, but they also need to be findable. If someone hears your name in a video and tries to search for it, will they spell it right? "chaoscasserole" is funny. "xXchaoscasseroleXx" is not, and nobody will ever type that into a search bar. Stick to real words or obvious combinations. Avoid numbers unless they're part of the joke.

If your name is taken, don't just slap numbers on the end. Try a period or underscore to separate words — `panic.mode` reads better than `panicmode47` and looks intentional instead of desperate. We wrote a whole guide on [what to do when your username is taken](/blog/username-already-taken) if you need more strategies.

If you're building a presence across multiple platforms, check whether your TikTok name is available on [Instagram, Twitter/X, and YouTube](/check/instagram) before you lock it in. Twitter's 15-character limit is the usual bottleneck, so check there first.

Getting known by a name that's taken everywhere else is a problem you don't want to solve later. Read the full breakdown of [character limits across platforms](/blog/username-character-limits) to avoid surprises.

One more thing: your username and your display name are different. The username is what people type to find you, what shows up in the URL, and what people tag in comments. The display name is what shows up on your profile and can include spaces, emoji, whatever. Don't confuse the two when picking a name from this list. These are all usernames — the part after the @.

Also worth noting: a funny username doesn't mean you can't be taken seriously. Some of the biggest creators on the platform got there with joke names and never changed them. The name gets you noticed. The content keeps people around. Pick something that makes you laugh, make sure it's available, and go make something worth watching.
