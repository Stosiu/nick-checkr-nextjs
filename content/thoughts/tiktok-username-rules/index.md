## The rules

TikTok usernames follow a specific set of constraints. Here's what you're working with:

- 2 to 24 characters long
- Letters (a-z), numbers (0-9), underscores (_), and periods (.)
- Cannot end with a period
- Case-insensitive: `CoolName` and `coolname` are treated as the same username
- Must be unique across all TikTok accounts

That's it. No spaces, no hyphens, no special characters, no emojis. The 24-character ceiling is shorter than [Instagram's](/check/instagram) 30-character limit but longer than [Twitter's](/check/twitter) tight 15-character cap. For most people, 24 characters is more than enough.

## How to change your TikTok username

1. Open TikTok and go to your profile
2. Tap **Edit profile**
3. Tap the **Username** field
4. Type your new username
5. Tap **Save**

TikTok checks availability instantly. If the name is free and follows the rules above, the change goes through immediately. No confirmation step, no email verification.

On desktop, the process is almost identical: go to your profile, click **Edit profile**, change the username field, and save. The web version has the same restrictions and the same 30-day cooldown.

## The 30-day cooldown

This is the part that catches people off guard. After you change your TikTok username, you cannot change it again for 30 days. Not 24 hours, not a week. A full month.

This means you should be confident in your choice before you commit. Test the name out loud. Type it a few times. Check if it's available on your other platforms. Because if you realize three days later that you misspelled something or just don't like how it looks, you're waiting 27 more days.

Compare this to Instagram, where you can [change your username](/blog/change-instagram-username) multiple times without a strict published cooldown, or Twitter, which lets you change anytime. TikTok is more locked down than both.

## What makes a username "unavailable"

You type in the perfect username, hit save, and TikTok says no. A few possible reasons:

Someone else has it. The obvious one. Even if the account looks inactive or has zero followers, the name is claimed. TikTok doesn't reclaim usernames from inactive accounts on any public timeline, so waiting it out is rarely a strategy.

It could also be reserved. TikTok holds back usernames tied to brands, trademarks, and public figures. You won't be able to register `@nike` or `@taylorswift` even if no active account has them.

Some usernames get blocked for violating community guidelines. Slurs, hate speech, and impersonation attempts are filtered automatically. Some borderline words get caught too, even if you didn't intend them offensively.

There's also a timing issue. After you change your username, TikTok holds the old one for a short period. During that window, nobody can claim it, including you if you changed your mind.

## Username vs display name on TikTok

TikTok has two name fields and people mix them up constantly.

Your **username** is the handle that starts with @. It appears in your profile URL (`tiktok.com/@username`), it's how people tag you in comments, and it follows all the rules above. Your **display name** is the bold text on your profile. It can be up to 30 characters, it allows spaces, and it's much more flexible.

You can have a display name of "John Smith" while your username is `@johnsmith_99`. The display name is cosmetic. The username is your identity.

This distinction matters when picking a name. Your username needs to be clean, short, and easy to type. Your display name is where you can add your real name, brand name, or anything else. ## Tips for picking a good TikTok username

### Keep it short

You have 24 characters. Use as few as possible. Short usernames are easier to remember, easier to type in search, and easier to fit in video watermarks when people share your content. Aim for under 15 characters if you can. Under 10 is even better.

### Skip the numbers at the end

Appending `_123` or `99` to a taken username is tempting but it makes the handle harder to remember and looks like you were late to the party. If `@alex` is taken, `@alex8847` isn't a great alternative. Consider a completely different approach instead.

### Think about how it sounds

TikTok is a video platform. People will say your username out loud in duets, stitches, and comments. If someone can't pronounce it or remember it after hearing it once, it's going to hurt discoverability. "Check out @thx_mngr_42" doesn't flow as well as "Check out @thanksmgr" in a video.

### Match your other platforms

If you already have established handles on Instagram, Twitter, or YouTube, use the same name on TikTok. Consistency across platforms makes you easier to find and builds recognition. ### Avoid periods at the end

This one's mechanical. TikTok doesn't allow usernames ending with a period, so you'll just get rejected. Periods in the middle of a username are fine (`first.last` works), but trailing periods are blocked.

### Don't use something you'll outgrow

If you're 16 and your username references your age, you're going to regret it in two years. If your handle is tied to a trend, it'll feel dated once the trend passes. Pick something that still works in five years.
