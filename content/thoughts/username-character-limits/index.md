## Why character limits matter

You found a username you like. You grab it on Instagram. Then you try Twitter — too long. You try Snapchat — can't use that period. You try Reddit — already locked in and can't change it even if you wanted to.

Every platform enforces its own rules about how long a username can be and which characters are allowed. If you're trying to [use the same username everywhere](/blog/same-username-everywhere), you need to know these rules before you commit.

This guide is a reference. Bookmark it. Come back to it when you're picking a new handle or checking whether your current one will work on a platform you're joining.

## Social media platforms

### Instagram

- **Length:** 1-30 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, periods, underscores
- **Case sensitivity:** No — `JohnDoe` and `johndoe` are the same account
- **Can you change it?** Yes, anytime from settings

Instagram is one of the more generous platforms. Thirty characters gives you plenty of room, and the ability to use periods and underscores means you have options if your first choice is [already taken](/blog/username-already-taken). One thing to note: your username and [display name are different things](/blog/username-vs-display-name). The display name can include spaces and special characters, but the username (the one after the @) follows these rules.

Check yours with the [Instagram username checker](/check/instagram).

### TikTok

- **Length:** 2-24 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores, periods
- **Restrictions:** Cannot end with a period
- **Can you change it?** Yes, but there's a 30-day cooldown between changes

TikTok's 24-character limit is slightly tighter than Instagram's but still comfortable for most usernames. The 30-day cooldown on changes means you should think carefully before switching — you're stuck with it for a month.

Check yours with the [TikTok username checker](/check/tiktok).

### Twitter / X

- **Length:** 1-15 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores only
- **Case sensitivity:** No
- **Can you change it?** Yes, anytime

Here's where things get tight. Fifteen characters sounds reasonable until you realize that millions of short, clean usernames were claimed in Twitter's first decade. No periods, no hyphens — just letters, numbers, and underscores. This is one of the most restrictive mainstream platforms, and it's often the bottleneck when you're trying to get a consistent handle everywhere.

The twitter handle character limit of 15 has been in place since launch and shows no signs of changing, even under new ownership.

Check yours with the [Twitter username checker](/check/twitter).

### Facebook

- **Length:** 5+ characters (no strict upper limit published, but custom URLs have practical limits)
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, periods
- **Case sensitivity:** No
- **Can you change it?** Yes, but Facebook limits how often

Facebook usernames are really about the custom URL (facebook.com/yourname). The minimum of 5 characters is higher than most platforms, which can be annoying if you use a short handle elsewhere.

### YouTube

- **Length:** 3-30 characters (handles)
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores, hyphens, periods
- **Can you change it?** Yes, with limitations on frequency

YouTube handles (the ones starting with @) were introduced in 2022. They're separate from your channel name and follow their own rules. The 30-character limit and broad set of allowed characters make YouTube one of the more flexible platforms.

### Snapchat

- **Length:** 3-15 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores
- **Restrictions:** Must start with a letter
- **Can you change it?** No — your Snapchat username is permanent

This is the big one. Snapchat does not let you change your username. Ever. Whatever you pick on signup is what you're stuck with. Combined with the 15-character limit and the requirement to start with a letter, you need to choose carefully. If you pick something you regret, your only option is creating a new account.

Check yours with the [Snapchat username checker](/check/snapchat).

### Reddit

- **Length:** 3-20 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores
- **Can you change it?** No — permanent, just like Snapchat

Reddit usernames are locked at creation. Twenty characters gives you decent room, and the allowed character set is straightforward. But since you can't change it, get it right the first time. Reddit does now offer display names that can be changed, but your u/username is forever.

Check yours with the [Reddit username checker](/check/reddit).

### Pinterest

- **Length:** 3-30 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores
- **Can you change it?** Yes

Pinterest is relatively relaxed. Thirty characters, a clean set of allowed characters, and the ability to change it if needed.

### Bluesky

- **Handle format:** username.bsky.social (or a custom domain)
- **Length:** The username portion follows domain label rules — 1-63 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, hyphens (no underscores)
- **Can you change it?** Yes — you can even use your own domain as your handle

Bluesky's handle system is unique. Your handle is technically a domain name, so the rules follow DNS conventions rather than typical username rules. No underscores, but hyphens are fine. The custom domain option means you could be @you.com instead of @you.bsky.social.

### Threads

- **Tied to Instagram** — your Threads username is your Instagram username
- **Same rules apply:** 1-30 characters, letters/numbers/periods/underscores

No separate username to worry about. Change it on Instagram and it changes on Threads.

## Developer platforms

### GitHub

- **Length:** Up to 39 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Alphanumeric and hyphens
- **Restrictions:** Cannot start or end with a hyphen, no consecutive hyphens
- **Can you change it?** Yes, but old URLs won't redirect forever

GitHub's 39-character limit is generous, and the restriction on consecutive hyphens rarely matters in practice. The bigger concern is that changing your username breaks existing links to your repositories, so developers tend to pick one early and stick with it.

Check yours with the [GitHub username checker](/check/github).

### GitLab

- **Length:** 2-255 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Alphanumeric, hyphens, underscores, periods
- **Restrictions:** Cannot start with a hyphen, cannot end with .git or .atom
- **Can you change it?** Yes

GitLab is the most permissive on length. 255 characters is more than you'll ever need. The rules are similar to GitHub but more relaxed.

### npm

- **Length:** 1-214 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Lowercase letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores, periods
- **Restrictions:** Must be lowercase
- **Can you change it?** Not easily — tied to your account

npm scopes (the @username part) follow these rules. The forced lowercase is worth knowing if your brand uses capital letters elsewhere.

### PyPI

- **Length:** No strict minimum published, practical maximum around 100 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, hyphens, underscores, periods
- **Restrictions:** Normalized — hyphens, underscores, and periods are treated as equivalent
- **Can you change it?** No — account names are fixed

PyPI's normalization is the standout rule here. `my-project`, `my_project`, and `my.project` all resolve to the same thing, which matters more for package names than usernames.

## Gaming platforms

### Steam

- **Custom URL:** Separate from display name
- **Length:** 2-32 characters for custom URL
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores
- **Can you change it?** Display name: yes, anytime. Custom URL: yes, but the old one becomes available again

Steam separates [username from display name](/blog/username-vs-display-name) more clearly than most platforms. Your display name is what people see in games, and you can change it whenever you want — even mid-match. The custom URL is for your profile page.

Check yours with the [Steam username checker](/check/steam).

### Twitch

- **Length:** 4-25 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, underscores
- **Can you change it?** Yes, every 60 days

Twitch's 4-character minimum is higher than most platforms. If you use a 3-letter handle elsewhere, you'll need at least one extra character here. The 60-day cooldown is more restrictive than Twitter but less than you might expect for a platform where your username is your brand.

Check yours with the [Twitch username checker](/check/twitch).

### Discord

- **Length:** 2-32 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Lowercase letters, numbers, underscores, periods
- **Restrictions:** Must be lowercase, no consecutive periods
- **Can you change it?** Yes

Discord overhauled its username system in 2023, dropping the old Name#1234 discriminator format. The new system enforces lowercase, which is unusual among major platforms. If your brand uses mixed case, Discord won't reflect that.

## Professional platforms

### LinkedIn

- **Custom URL:** 3-100 characters
- **Allowed characters:** Letters, numbers, hyphens
- **Can you change it?** Yes, up to 5 times within 6 months

LinkedIn's custom URL is more about SEO than identity. Most people use some variation of their real name. The 100-character limit is effectively unlimited for practical purposes.

## The universal safe zone

Looking at the table, a pattern shows up. If you want one username that works everywhere without modification:

- **Length:** 4-15 characters. Four covers Twitch's minimum. Fifteen covers Twitter's maximum and Snapchat's ceiling.
- **Characters:** Lowercase letters and numbers only. Every platform supports these. Underscores are close to universal but fail on GitHub and LinkedIn. Hyphens fail on Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter. Periods are inconsistent everywhere.
- **Start with a letter.** Snapchat requires it, and it's good practice regardless.
- **Avoid trailing periods.** TikTok blocks them, and they look odd on every other platform too.

The sweet spot: **a 4-15 character handle using only lowercase letters and numbers, starting with a letter.** That's your safest bet for claiming a consistent identity across every major platform.
